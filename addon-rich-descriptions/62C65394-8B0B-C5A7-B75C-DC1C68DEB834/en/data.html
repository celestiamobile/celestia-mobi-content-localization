The Rosette Nebula, also known as NGC 2237 and Caldwell 49, is a distant H-II star-forming region about 5,500 light-years away in the constellation Monoceros. The entire complex is about 130 light-years across and about 2,500 young stars were found within its hydrogen clouds. In 2019, Oklahoma declared this nebula as their state astronomical object.

Note

This addon also includes the open cluster called NGC 2244 and the stars associated with it.
