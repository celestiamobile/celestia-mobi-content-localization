The Galileo Navigational Satellite System (GNSS) is the European equivalent to the US Global Positioning System (GPS). Its constellation of 34 satellites (26 active, 8 derelict) provide Europe with high-precision geodesic navigation without relying much on GPS and GLONASS satellites. First launch occurred on December 2005

Note

Not to be confused with NASA's Galileo mission, which studied Jupiter and its moons from 1995 to 2003
