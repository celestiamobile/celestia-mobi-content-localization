2024 YR₄ is an Apollo-type, near-Earth asteroid discovered on 2024-Dec-27. It initially had a considerable chance of colliding with Earth in 2032-Dec-22, when it reached Level 3 on the Torino scale (1-in-32 in 2025-Feb-18), the highest of any asteroid since Apophis in 2004. Subsequent observations have reduced the risk of an Earth impact to effectively zero, however it still has a 1-in-58 chance of colliding with the Moon. Due to the large uncertainties, additional observations will still be needed to further refine its orbit. 2024 YR4 is about 60 meters in diameter, and light curves indicate that it has an elongated shape and a fast rotation period of only 19.5 minutes.

Note

The asteroid's 3D model is based on light-curve observations taken by the Gemini South Telescope on 7 February 2025, which were publicly released on 30 March 2025

Nominal closest approach to the Moon in 2032-Dec-22: 3,700 km

Nominal closest approach to Earth in 2032-Dec-22: 255,600 km
