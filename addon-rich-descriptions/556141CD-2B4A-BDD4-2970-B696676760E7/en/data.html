208996 Achlys, formerly known as 2003 AZ₈₄, is an elongated 940-km trans-Neptunian object that crosses the orbit of Neptune. It is the third largest plutino after Pluto itself and Orcus. One unconfirmed satellite might be orbiting Achlys. It is named after the goddess of sorrow and grief in Greek mythology
